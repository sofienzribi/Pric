1)Application


The Accountants Professional Liability (APL) may be written to provide coverage to risks that meet the following underwriting criteria:
1. Applicant(s) must be a licensed Certified Public Accountant or accounting firm
2. Excluding big 5 accounting firm in the world
3. Applicant must complete and sign an application for insurance and supplemental application(s) if applicable

2)Policy term

Policies are written for a term up to one year, plus odd time up to a maximum of 18 months
All premiums and rates contained in this manual are annual, unless otherwise specifically identified.
Each renewal or anniversay premium shall be computed according to the then current rules, rates and forms

3)Rounding procedure


Round any amount involving $ .50 or over to the next higher whole dollar
Round any amount involving $ 0.49 or less to the next lower whole dollar

4) Factors or Multipliers


Unless otherwise indicated in this manual, factors or multipliers are to be applied consecutively and not added together

5)Additional premium changes


a. Pro rate all changes requiring additional premium
b. Apply the rates and rules in effect on the effective date of the policy. In computing the additional premium,
charge the amount applicable on the effective date of the change
c. Waive additional premium of $ 15.00 or less.

6)Return Premium Changes (for other than policy cancellations)


A. Deletion of any coverage, other than optional coverages, is not permitted unless the entire policy is cancelled
b. Compute return premium at the rates used to calculate the policy premium at policy inception
c. Compute return premium pro rata and round to the next higher whole dollar when any coverage or exposure
is deleted or an amount of insurance is reduced
d. Waive return premium of $ 15.00 or less. Grant any return premium due if requested by the insured.

7)Limit of Liability


A. Basic Limits : $ 250,000 each claim and aggregate
Manual rates provide a basic limit of $ 250,000 for each claim that is first made against the insured during the policy period by reason of an act or omission in the performance of legal services by the insured or any person for whom the insured is legally liable, regardless of the number of claims or claimants, subject to a basic aggregate limit of $ 250,000
B. Increased Limits
Refer to the Rule D.1 for available increased limits and factors

8)Policy Cancellations


a. Policies are non-cancellable except for the following reasons:
1. the policy is cancelled at the Company's request for non-payment of premium
2. the policy is cancelled at the insured's request.
b. Compute the return premium at 0.90 of the pro rata unearned premium for the one year period and round in accordance with the rounding rule

9)Deductibles

Rates contained in this manual are based upon a $ 2,500 aggregate deductible. Refer to Rule D.2 for deductible factors
